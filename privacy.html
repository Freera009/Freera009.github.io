2.2 Data we collect and why

a) Server log data (automatic). When you access our website, your browser automatically sends information to our hosting provider. We log:

  • IP address (truncated where possible)
  • Date and time of access
  • Pages visited, HTTP status, bytes transferred
  • Referring URL (where you came from)
  • Browser type, version, and operating system

Legal basis: Article 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest in secure and stable website operation).
Retention: Log data is deleted after 30 days unless required for security investigation.
Hosting provider: This website is hosted on GitHub Pages (GitHub, Inc., 88 Colin P. Kelly Jr. Street, San Francisco, CA 94107, USA). Data transfer to the USA is covered by the EU-US Data Privacy Framework. GitHub's privacy practices: GitHub Privacy Statement.

b) Contact form and direct email. When you send us an enquiry via the contact form or by email, we collect:

  • Your name
  • Your email address
  • Your company and company size (optional)
  • Your area of interest
  • The content of your message

Legal basis: Article 6(1)(b) GDPR (pre-contractual measures at your request) and Article 6(1)(a) GDPR (consent, for optional fields). For existing business relationships, Article 6(1)(f) GDPR applies.
Purpose: To respond to your enquiry and, where applicable, to discuss or deliver our services.
Retention: Enquiry data is retained for as long as necessary to answer your request and for up to 3 years thereafter (limitation period under § 195 BGB). Contract-related data is retained for up to 10 years in accordance with German tax and commercial law (§ 257 HGB, § 147 AO).
Form processor: Form submissions are processed via Formspree (Formspree Inc., 2810 N Church Street #60333, Wilmington, DE 19802-4447, USA). Data transfer to the USA is covered by the EU-US Data Privacy Framework. See Formspree's Privacy Policy.

c) Booking a call. If you book a call via our scheduling link, you will be directed to our scheduling provider (Cal.com or equivalent), which collects your name, email address, and chosen time slot. Their privacy policy applies to that processing.

Legal basis: Article 6(1)(b) GDPR.
Retention: Scheduling records are kept for the duration of the business relationship and up to 3 years thereafter.

2.6 Your rights under GDPR

You have the following rights with regard to your personal data:

  • Right of access (Article 15 GDPR) — to know what data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification (Article 16 GDPR) — to have inaccurate data corrected.
  • Right to erasure / "right to be forgotten" (Article 17 GDPR) — to have your data deleted.
  • Right to restriction of processing (Article 18 GDPR).
  • Right to data portability (Article 20 GDPR) — to receive your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Right to object (Article 21 GDPR) — to object to processing based on legitimate interest.
  • Right to withdraw consent (Article 7(3) GDPR) — where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of previous processing.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at contact@freeraconsulting.com. We will respond within one month as required by Article 12(3) GDPR.
